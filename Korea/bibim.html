Bibimbap

Bibimbap (비빔밥) is one of the most well-known and beloved Korean dishes, recognised for its vibrant colours and balanced nutrition. The name literally means “mixed rice,” reflecting the way the dish is combined before eating.

This comforting meal consists of warm rice topped with seasoned vegetables, savoury meat, and a fried egg, finished with gochujang (Korean chilli paste). When mixed together, it creates a harmonious blend of spicy, savoury, slightly sweet, and nutty flavours.

Bibimbap (비빔밥) – Home Recipe

Ingredients

For the Meat

  • Beef mince – 100 g
  • Soy sauce – 1 tablespoon
  • Brown sugar – 1 teaspoon
  • Minced garlic – ¼ teaspoon
  • Cooking oil – 1 teaspoon

For the Vegetables

  • Seasoned spinach – 250 g
  • Seasoned bean sprouts – 350 g
  • Shiitake mushrooms – 100 g
  • Carrots – 120 g (julienned)
  • Fine sea salt – ½ teaspoon
  • Cooking oil – as needed

For the Rice and Toppings

  • Steamed white rice – 3 to 4 servings
  • Eggs – 3 to 4
  • Toasted shredded seasoned seaweed – optional

For the Bibimbap Sauce

  • Gochujang – 2 tablespoons
  • Sesame oil – 1 tablespoon
  • Sugar – 1 tablespoon
  • Water – 1 tablespoon
  • Roasted sesame seeds – 1 tablespoon
  • Vinegar – 1 teaspoon
  • Minced garlic – 1 teaspoon

How to Make Bibimbap (Step-by-Step)

Prepare the Meat

  • Step 1: Marinate the Beef
    • Mix beef, soy sauce, sugar, and garlic.
    • Marinate for about 30 minutes.

  • Step 2: Cook the Beef
    • Heat oil over medium-high heat.
    • Stir-fry beef for 3–5 minutes until cooked.

Prepare the Vegetables

  • Step 3: Prepare Spinach and Bean Sprouts
    • Cook and season separately.

  • Step 4: Cook the Carrots
    • Stir-fry with salt for 2–3 minutes.

  • Step 5: Cook the Mushrooms
    • Stir-fry sliced mushrooms until tender.

Sauce and Eggs

  • Step 6: Make the Sauce
    • Mix all sauce ingredients together.

  • Step 7: Fry the Eggs
    • Fry eggs sunny-side up.

Serve

  • Step 8: Assemble the Bowl
    • Place rice in a bowl.
    • Arrange toppings neatly.
    • Add egg and sauce.

  • Step 9: Mix and Enjoy
    • Mix well before eating.

Tip: Lightly season each ingredient on its own, then adjust the flavour with gochujang when mixing to maintain balance.

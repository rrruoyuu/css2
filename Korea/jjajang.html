Jjajangmyeon

Jjajangmyeon is a beloved Korean noodle dish made with thick wheat noodles topped with a savory black bean sauce cooked with diced pork or seafood and vegetables like onions, zucchini, and mushrooms. The sauce is rich, slightly sweet, and coats the noodles perfectly, creating a comforting, hearty meal.

This dish is especially associated with Korean Black Day (April 14), when singles gather to eat jjajangmyeon together and share their feelings in a lighthearted way.

Jjajangmyeon – Home Recipe

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 1 kg fresh Jjajangmyeon noodles
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 medium zucchini
  • 2 large potatoes
  • 7 large button mushrooms
  • ¼ small cabbage
  • 600 g diced pork

Pork Marinade Sauce

  • 1 Tbsp rice wine (mirin)
  • 5 sprinkles ground salt
  • 5 sprinkles ground black pepper
  • ¼ tsp ginger powder

Korean Black Bean Sauce

  • 6 Tbsp Chunjang (Korean black bean paste)
  • 90 g lard
  • 2 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 4 Tbsp rice wine (mirin)
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • 1 cup water
  • Slurry: 5 Tbsp potato starch + 4 Tbsp water

Toppings

  • 1 large cucumber, julienned
  • Hard-boiled egg, sliced

How to Make Jjajangmyeon (Step-by-Step)

Preparation

  • Step 1: Marinate the Pork
    • Rinse the pork under cold water and pat dry.
    • Mix the pork with the marinade sauce and let it sit for 15 minutes.

  • Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables
    • Dice onion, zucchini, and potatoes into small cubes.
    • Slice mushrooms and cabbage into small pieces.
    • Julienne cucumber for garnish.

Cooking

  • Step 3: Cook the Black Bean Sauce
    • Preheat a wok, add lard or cooking oil to melt.
    • Add black bean paste and stir over medium heat for 3–5 minutes.
    • Add brown sugar and stir 2–3 minutes.
    • Scoop out the paste (without oil) and set aside, leaving oil in the wok.

  • Step 4: Cook the Pork and Vegetables
    • Add pork to wok and cook until halfway done.
    • Add onion, zucchini, and potato, cook 3–5 minutes.
    • Add mushrooms and cabbage, stir 2–3 minutes.
    • Return black bean paste to the wok and mix for 1–2 minutes.

  • Step 5: Add Liquid
    • Add chicken stock, water, and rice wine; simmer 5–7 minutes.
    • Add slurry to thicken the sauce.

  • Step 6: Cook the Noodles
    • Boil noodles 3–5 minutes, rinse under cold water, drain, and place in serving bowls.

  • Step 7: Assemble and Serve
    • Pour black bean sauce over noodles.
    • Top with cucumber and sliced hard-boiled egg.
    • Mix well before eating. Serve immediately with optional yellow pickled radish.

Tip: Serve jjajangmyeon hot and mix the noodles well with the sauce for the best texture. Avoid letting noodles sit too long, or they will become too soft.

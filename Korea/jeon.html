Kimchi Jeon (Kimchi Pancakes)

Kimchi pancakes, or kimchi jeon (김치전), are a popular Korean savory dish made by mixing chopped kimchi with a simple batter of flour, water, and sometimes egg, then pan-frying until crispy on the outside and tender inside. They are slightly spicy, tangy, and savory, with umami flavor from the fermented kimchi. Often served with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, and a touch of sugar.

Kimchi Jeon (김치전) – Home Recipe

Ingredients

For the Pancake

  • 1 cup Buchimgaru (Korean pancake mix) or all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup ice-cold water
  • 1 cup well-fermented kimchi, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon kimchi liquid
  • 1 teaspoon gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • Cooking oil, as needed

For the Dipping Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons Soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Vinegar
  • ½ teaspoon Sugar
  • Optional: sesame seeds or chopped scallions

How to Make Kimchi Jeon (Step-by-Step)

Prepare the Batter

  • Step 1: Mix Ingredients
    • Combine pancake mix and water until smooth and lump-free.
    • Stir in chopped kimchi, kimchi liquid, and gochugaru until evenly mixed.

Cook the Pancake

  • Step 2: Heat Pan
    • Heat a pan over medium-high heat and add cooking oil to coat evenly.

  • Step 3: Pour and Shape
    • Pour batter into the pan and spread into a 5–6 inch round pancake.

  • Step 4: Cook Until Crispy
    • Cook 10–20 seconds on medium-high, then reduce heat to medium.
    • Cook until golden and crispy on the edges.
    • Flip when the bottom is golden and top has set, press gently for crispiness.

  • Step 5: Serve
    • Transfer to a cutting board, slice into bite-sized pieces, and serve immediately with dipping sauce.

Tip: Keep the batter thin and spread evenly to get crispy edges. Adjust oil as needed for golden, crunchy pancakes.

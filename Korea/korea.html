Four Flavours

Korean Cuisine

Korean cuisine is known for its bold flavors, balance, and emphasis on shared meals. It often features a mix of spicy, savory, sweet, and fermented tastes, with ingredients like chili paste (gochujang), soybean paste (doenjang), garlic, and sesame oil playing a big role. Many dishes, such as kimchi, bulgogi, and bibimbap, highlight fermented foods, which add depth of flavor and are an important part of Korean food culture. Korean meals are usually served with multiple side dishes called banchan, encouraging variety and communal eating.

In this food blog, we will list easy and authentic Korean recipes that are simple to follow at home. Each recipe will use traditional ingredients while keeping the steps clear and beginner-friendly, making Korean cooking accessible to everyone.

1. Bibimbap (Mixed Rice)

Bibimbap (비빔밥) is one of the most well-known and beloved Korean dishes, enjoyed for its vibrant appearance and balanced flavors. It is a bowl of warm rice topped with a variety of seasoned vegetables, such as spinach, bean sprouts, carrots, and mushrooms, often served with sliced meat and a fried or raw egg on top. The dish is mixed together with gochujang (Korean chili paste) before eating, creating a flavorful combination of spicy, savory, slightly sweet, and nutty tastes. Bibimbap is known for its harmony of textures as well, blending soft rice, crisp vegetables, and rich egg into a satisfying and comforting meal.

Ingredients for Bibimbap (Serves 3 to 4)

Meat and Meat Sauce

  • 100g Beef mince (or other cuts)
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 1/4 tsp minced garlice

Vegetables and Others

  • 250g Seasoned Spinach
  • 350g Seasoned Bean Sprouts
  • 100g Shiitake Mushroom
  • 120g Carrots
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 3 to 4 Serving Portions of Steamed Rice
  • 3 to 4 Eggs
  • Cooking Oil
  • Toasted Shredded Seasoned Seaweed

Bibimbap Sauce

  • 2 Tbsp Gochujang
  • 1 Tbsp Seasame oil
  • 1 Tbsp Sugar
  • 1 Tbsp Water
  • 1 Tbsp Roasted Sesame Seeds
  • 1 tsp Vinegar
  • 1 tsp Minced Garlic

How to Make Bibimbap

1. Prepare and cook the ingredients

  • For the meat, combine the beef mince with the meat sauce listed above. Let it marinate for about 30 minutes while you prepare the other ingredients to deepen the flavor. Heat some cooking oil in a wok over medium-high to high heat, then add the marinated meat and cook for about 3 to 5 minutes, stirring until it is fully cooked.
  • Mix the bibimbap sauce ingredients listed above in a bowl.
  • Cook the spinach and bean sprouts.
  • Wash, peel, and cut the carrots into thin strips. Heat some cooking oil in a wok, add the carrots along with 1/4 teaspoon of fine sea salt, and stir-fry over medium-high to high heat for about 2 to 3 minutes until just tender.
  • Rinse the shiitake mushrooms and slice them thinly. Heat some cooking oil in a wok, add the mushrooms along with 1/4 teaspoon of fine sea salt, and cook over medium-high to high heat until they are fully cooked, about 2 to 3 minutes.
  • Make fried eggs

2. Place the rice in a bowl, then arrange the cooked meat, mixed vegetables, seasoned seaweed, and bibimbap sauce on top. Finish by adding the egg over the rice and serve

3. To eat, mix the ingredients in the bowl and enjoy!

2. Tteokbokki (Stir-Fried Rice Cake)

Tteokbokki is one of the most popular Korean street foods in Korea, loved for its bold flavor and comforting texture. It is made with chewy cylindrical rice cakes simmered in a thick, spicy sauce made from gochujang, soy sauce, and sugar. Fish cakes, boiled eggs, and vegetables are often added to enhance the dish. Tteokbokki has a rich, spicy, and slightly sweet taste, with a soft yet chewy bite that makes it especially satisfying and addictive, especially when served hot.

Ingredients for Tteokbokki (Serves 2)

Main

  • 350g Korean Rice Cakes
  • 150g Korean Fish Cakes
  • 2 Cups of Korean Soup Stock (dried kelp and dried anchovy stock)
  • 60g Onion, thinly sliced

Tteokbokki Sauce

  • 3 Tbsp Gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp raw sugar
  • 1 tsp Soy Sauce
  • 1 tsp Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)

Garnish

  • 1 tsp Toasted Sesame Seeds
  • 1 tsp Sesame Oil
  • 1 Stalk Green Onion, finely chopped

How to make Tteokbokki

1. Soak the rice cakes in warm water for about 10 minutes. If you are using fresh rice cakes that are already soft, you can skip this step. However, for packaged rice cakes stored in the refrigerator, soaking is recommended to improve their moisture and texture before cooking.

2. Bring the soup stock to a boil in a shallow pot over medium-high heat, then stir in the tteokbokki sauce until it is fully dissolved. Once the seasoned stock is boiling, add the rice cakes, fish cakes, and onion, and continue cooking for another 3 to 5 minutes until the rice cakes are tender. Lower the heat and let it simmer for an additional 2 to 4 minutes to thicken the sauce and enhance the flavor.

3. Add the sesame oil, sesame seeds, and green onion then quickly stir. Serve warm.

3. Gimbap (Seaweed Rice Roll)

Kimbap is a popular picnic and everyday food in Korea, known for its convenience, colorful appearance, and balanced flavors. It is made by rolling cooked rice seasoned with sesame oil and salt together with a variety of fillings such as vegetables, egg, pickled radish, and sometimes meat or seafood, all wrapped in dried seaweed. Kimbap has a mild, savory, and slightly nutty taste, with a mix of soft rice, crisp vegetables, and flavorful fillings, making it light, satisfying, and easy to enjoy on the go.

Ingredients for Kimbap (Makes 4 rolls)

  • 4 Dried Seaweed Sheets
  • 120g Spinach
  • 2 Eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 Carrot, julienned
  • 2 to 4 Imitation Crab Sticks, cut in half length ways
  • 4 sticks BBQ Kimbap Ham, cut into long strips
  • 4 yellow Radish Pickle, cut into long strips
  • 2 1/2 cups Steamed short Grain Rice
  • 2 Tbsp Sesame Oil
  • 3/8 tsp Fine Sea Salt

How to make Kimbap

1. Rinse the spinach and briefly blanch it in rapidly boiling water for about 30 seconds to 1 minute. Drain, rinse with cold water, and gently squeeze out any excess moisture. Place the spinach in a bowl, add 1/8 teaspoon of salt and 1/2 tablespoon of sesame oil, then mix well.

2. Preheat a pan or skillet with a small amount of cooking oil. Pour in the beaten egg and cook over medium heat until both sides are fully set. Transfer the omelette to a cutting board and slice it into long strips.

3. Using a heated pan over medium heat, lightly cook the carrots, crab sticks, and kimbap ham separately, about 1 minute per ingredient. (These can be used raw, but lightly cooking them adds more flavor.)

4. Place the cooked rice in a mixing bowl and season it with 1/2 tablespoon of sesame oil and 1/4 teaspoon of fine sea salt, adjusting to taste. Mix gently with a rice spatula.

5. Arrange all the prepared ingredients at a kimbap rolling station to make assembly easier. Place a sheet of dried seaweed, shiny side down, on a bamboo mat. Spread the rice evenly and thinly over about two-thirds of the seaweed, leaving a 5 cm (2-inch) gap at the top.

6. Layer the filling ingredients evenly across the rice. For example, place a strip of yellow pickled radish about one-third up the seaweed, then add burdock, ham, and a crab stick, followed by egg strips, carrots, and spinach. Roll the seaweed tightly using the mat.

7. If the seaweed does not seal, lightly moisten the exposed edge with water or press a few grains of rice onto it. Set the finished roll aside and repeat the process with the remaining ingredients.

8. Once all the kimbap rolls are ready, place them on a cutting board and lightly brush the outside with about 1 tablespoon of sesame oil. Slice into bite-sized pieces and serve.

4. Jjajangmyeon (Black Bean Noodles)

Jjajangmyeon is a popular Korean noodle dish made with thick wheat noodles topped with a rich black bean sauce cooked with diced pork or seafood and vegetables such as onions and zucchini. The sauce is savory, slightly sweet, and deeply flavorful, with a smooth and hearty texture that coats the noodles well.

Jjajangmyeon is especially associated with Korean Black Day on April 14, when single people who did not receive gifts on Valentine’s Day (February 14) or White Day (March 14) gather to eat this dish together and share their feelings in a lighthearted way.

Ingredients for Jjajangmyeon (Serves 6)

Main

  • 1kg Fresh Jajangmyeon Noodles
  • Large Onion
  • 1 Medium Zucchini
  • 2 Large Potatoes
  • 7 Large Button Mushrooms
  • 1/4 Small Cabbage
  • 600g Diced Pork

Pork Marinade Sauce

  • 1 Tbsp Rice Wine (Mirin)
  • 5 Sprinkles Ground Salt
  • 5 Sprinkles Ground Black Pepper
  • 1/4 tsp Ginger Powder

Korean Black Bean Sauce

  • 6 Tbsp Chunjang (Korean black bean paste)
  • 90g Lard
  • 2 Tbsp Brown Sugar
  • 4 Tbsp Rice Wine (Mirin)
  • 1 cup Chicken Stock
  • 1 cup Water
  • Slurry (Potato starch 5 Tbsp + water 4 Tbsp)

Toppings

  • 1 Large Cucumber
  • Hard Boiled Egg

How to make Jjajangmyeon

Prepartion:

1. Rinse the pork under cold water and pat it dry with paper towels. Coat the pork with the marinade sauce, mix well, and let it sit for 15 minutes.

2. Cut the onion, zucchini, and potato into small cubes, thinly slice the mushrooms, chop the cabbage into small to medium pieces, and julienne the cucumber.

Cooking:

1. Preheat a wok until it is hot, then add lard or cooking oil and let it melt.

2. Add the black bean paste and stir constantly over medium heat for 3–5 minutes, being careful not to burn it.

3. Add the brown sugar and stir for another 2-3 minutes.

4. Scoop out the paste (without the oil) and set it aside, leaving the oil in the wok for the next steps.

5. Add the pork to the wok and cook until it is halfway done.

6. Stir in the onion, zucchini, and potato and cook for 3–5 minutes until they are about one-third cooked.

7. Add the mushrooms and cabbage and stir for 2–3 minutes.

8. Return the black bean paste to the wok and mix it with the vegetables, cooking for 1–2 minutes.

9. Add the chicken stock, water, and rice wine, then simmer on medium heat for 5–7 minutes. (Covering the wok will speed up cooking.)

10. Add the slurry to thicken the sauces

11. While the sauce simmers, boil water in a separate pot and cook the noodles for 3–5 minutes. Rinse them under cold water, drain, and place in a serving bowl or plate.

12. Pour the black bean sauce over the noodles. Optionally, garnish with cucumber slices or a hard-boiled egg.

13. Mix the noodles and sauce well with chopsticks and enjoy. Traditionally, jjajangmyeon is served with yellow pickled radish, which aids digestion. Eat promptly before the noodles become too soft.

5. Kimchi Jeon (Kimchi Pancakes)

Kimchi pancakes, or kimchi jeon (김치전), are a popular Korean savory dish made by mixing chopped kimchi with a simple batter of flour, water, and sometimes egg, then pan-frying it until crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. They are slightly spicy, tangy, and savory, with the fermented kimchi giving a rich, umami flavor. The crispy edges combined with the tender, flavorful interior make kimchi pancakes a satisfying snack or side dish, often enjoyed with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, and a touch of sugar.

Ingredients for Kimchi Jeon

  • 1 cup Buchimgaru (Korean pancake mix) or plain all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup Icy Cold Water
  • 1 cup Well-Fermented Kimchi, chopped in bite-sized pieces
  • 1 Tbsp Kimchi Liquid
  • 1 tsp Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • Cooking Oil
  • Homemade Korean Pancake Dipping Sauce

How to make Kimchi Jeon

  • 1. In a medium bowl, mix the buchimgaru (Korean pancake mix) with water until the batter is smooth and lump-free. Stir in the chopped kimchi, kimchi juice, and finely ground Korean chili flakes until everything is evenly combined.
  • 2. Heat a pan or skillet over medium-high heat until hot, then add a generous amount of cooking oil and swirl to coat the surface evenly.
  • 3. Using a ladle, pour the batter into the pan, spreading it gently with the back of the ladle into a thin, round pancake about 5–6 inches wide. This size helps it cook evenly, crisp up at the edges, and makes flipping easier.
  • 4. Cook the pancake over medium-high heat for 10–20 seconds to set the bottom, then lower the heat to medium. Continue cooking until it’s golden, crispy on the outside, and cooked through. If the pan gets too hot or the pancake browns too quickly, reduce the heat slightly. Add more oil as needed to keep it crispy and prevent sticking.
  • 5. When the pancake is about 70% cooked, flip it carefully once the bottom is golden and the top has started to set. Press gently with a spatula a few times to enhance crispiness.
  • 6. Once both sides are golden and crispy, transfer the pancake to a cutting board, slice it into bite-sized pieces, and serve immediately. A small dish of Korean pancake dipping sauce makes the perfect accompaniment.

Tteokbokki

Tteokbokki (떡볶이) is one of Korea’s most popular street foods, loved for its chewy rice cakes in a rich, spicy-sweet sauce. Often paired with fish cakes, boiled eggs, and vegetables, it’s a comforting dish best served hot.

Its bold flavours and satisfying texture make it a favorite snack or meal, perfect for sharing with friends or enjoying on a cozy night in.

Tteokbokki (떡볶이) – Home Recipe

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 350g Korean rice cakes
  • 150g Korean fish cakes
  • 2 cups Korean soup stock (dried kelp & dried anchovy)
  • 60g Onion, thinly sliced

Sauce

  • 3 Tbsp Gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 ½ Tbsp Raw sugar
  • 1 tsp Soy sauce
  • 1 tsp Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)

Garnish

  • 1 tsp Toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tsp Sesame oil
  • 1 Stalk green onion, finely chopped

How to Make Tteokbokki (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Prepare the Rice Cakes

  • Soak the rice cakes in warm water for 10 minutes (skip if fresh and soft). This helps refrigerated rice cakes regain moisture.

Step 2: Make the Sauce

  • Bring the soup stock to a boil over medium-high heat.
  • Stir in gochujang, sugar, soy sauce, and gochugaru until fully dissolved.

Step 3: Cook the Tteokbokki

  • Add rice cakes, fish cakes, and onion to the boiling stock.
  • Cook 3–5 minutes until rice cakes are tender.
  • Lower heat and simmer 2–4 more minutes to thicken the sauce.

Step 4: Garnish and Serve

  • Stir in sesame oil, sprinkle with sesame seeds, and top with chopped green onion.
  • Serve warm and enjoy!

Tip: Adjust the spiciness and sweetness according to your taste. For a milder version, reduce the gochugaru or sugar slightly.

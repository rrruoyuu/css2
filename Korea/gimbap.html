Bite of Asia

Gimbap

Gimbap (김밥) is a popular everyday and picnic food in Korea, loved for its convenience, colourful appearance, and balanced flavours. It is made by rolling seasoned rice together with various fillings and wrapping them in dried seaweed.

With its mild, savoury, and slightly nutty taste, gimbap offers a pleasant mix of soft rice, crisp vegetables, and flavourful fillings, making it light, satisfying, and easy to enjoy on the go.

Gimbap (김밥) – Home Recipe

Ingredients

For the Rice

  • Short-grain rice – 2½ cups (steamed)
  • Sesame oil – 2 tablespoons
  • Fine sea salt – ⅜ teaspoon

For the Fillings

  • Dried seaweed sheets – 4
  • Spinach – 120 g
  • Eggs – 2 (beaten)
  • Carrot – ½ (julienned)
  • Imitation crab sticks – 2 to 4 (halved lengthwise)
  • BBQ gimbap ham – 4 strips
  • Pickled yellow radish – 4 strips

How to Make Gimbap (Step-by-Step)

Prepare the Ingredients

  • Step 1: Prepare the Spinach
    • Blanch spinach in boiling water for 30 seconds to 1 minute.
    • Rinse with cold water, squeeze dry, and season with salt and sesame oil.

  • Step 2: Cook the Egg
    • Cook beaten eggs into a thin omelette.
    • Slice into long strips.

  • Step 3: Prepare the Other Fillings
    • Lightly cook carrots, crab sticks, and ham separately.
    • Set aside with pickled radish.

Prepare the Rice

  • Step 4: Season the Rice
    • Mix steamed rice with sesame oil and salt.
    • Gently fold to avoid crushing the grains.

Assemble and Roll

  • Step 5: Assemble the Gimbap
    • Place seaweed shiny side down on a bamboo mat.
    • Spread rice thinly over two-thirds of the sheet.
    • Arrange fillings evenly across the rice.

  • Step 6: Roll and Slice
    • Roll tightly using the bamboo mat.
    • Brush the outside with sesame oil.
    • Slice into bite-sized pieces and serve.

Tip: Avoid overfilling the gimbap—keeping the fillings balanced ensures neat rolls and makes slicing much easier.

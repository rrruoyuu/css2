Peking Duck

Peking Duck is one of the most iconic dishes in Chinese cuisine, celebrated for its paper-thin crispy skin and fragrant, juicy meat. Originating from Beijing, this dish is often associated with special occasions and shared meals, where the duck is expertly roasted and sliced before diners. If you want to experience an authentic version, well-known restaurants such as Quanjude and Da Dong Roast Duck are famous for their mastery of this classic dish, offering generations-old techniques and unforgettable flavours.

Beyond its cultural significance, Peking Duck is also a dish you can try making at home. From the crispy skin to the balance of hoisin sauce, cucumber, and spring onions wrapped in soft pancakes, each bite is deeply satisfying. Check out the cooking guide to learn how to prepare Peking Duck and recreate this classic dish in your own kitchen!

Peking Duck – Home Recipe

Ingredients

For the Duck:

  • 1 whole duck (about 2–2.5 kg), cleaned
  • 1 tbsp honey or maltose
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1 cup hot water

For Serving:

  • Thin Chinese pancakes (or tortillas as a substitute)
  • Hoisin sauce
  • Spring onions (julienned)
  • Cucumber (julienned)

How to Make

  • Step 1: Prepare the Duck
    • Rinse the duck thoroughly and pat it dry with paper towels
    • Make sure the skin is completely dry to help it turn crispy

  • Step 2: Crisp the Skin
    • Mix honey (or maltose), vinegar, and hot water in a bowl
    • Brush the mixture evenly over the duck skin
    • Hang or place the duck uncovered in the fridge for 8–24 hours to air-dry

  • Step 3: Roast the Duck
    • Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F)
    • Place the duck on a rack with a tray underneath to catch the fat
    • Roast for about 60–75 minutes, turning once, until the skin is golden and crispy

  • Step 4: Rest and Slice
    • Remove the duck from the oven and let it rest for 10 minutes
    • Slice off the crispy skin and tender meat into thin pieces

  • Step 5: Serve
    • Spread hoisin sauce on a pancake
    • Add duck, spring onion, and cucumber
    • Roll it up and enjoy while warm

HotPot

Hotpot is a popular and social style of dining in China, where fresh ingredients are cooked together in a pot of simmering broth placed at the centre of the table. Common ingredients include thinly sliced meats, seafood, vegetables, tofu, and noodles, allowing diners to cook food to their own preference. Hotpot is especially enjoyed during colder seasons and is known for bringing family and friends together through shared meals and lively conversations.

China is home to many hotpot styles, each with its own unique flavours. Two of the most well-known are Chongqing hotpot, famous for its spicy and numbing broth, and Beijing hotpot, which features a lighter, clearer soup that highlights the natural taste of the ingredients. Explore these popular hotpot varieties to discover the different flavours and traditions behind this beloved Chinese dining experience.

ChongQing HotPot

Chongqing hotpot is one of the most famous and distinctive hotpot styles in China, known for its rich, fiery broth and bold flavours. Originating from Chongqing in southwestern China, this hotpot is characterised by a deep red soup base made with dried chillies, Sichuan peppercorns, garlic, and spices, creating a taste that is both spicy and numbing. The broth is usually simmered with beef tallow, giving it a strong aroma and intense flavour.

Diners cook a wide variety of ingredients in the bubbling broth, such as thinly sliced beef, pork, vegetables, tofu, and noodles. Chongqing hotpot is especially popular for its exciting heat and warming effect, making it a favourite choice for those who enjoy spicy food and lively shared meals with friends and family. Chongqing Hot Pot – Home Recipe

Ingredients

Hot Pot Base:

  • 1 pack Qiaotou hot pot seasoning (recommend)
  • Water (boiling)
  • Optional: sesame paste, Lao Gan Ma (to adjust flavour)

Meat (Choose Any):

  • Thinly sliced frozen lamb
  • Fatty beef slices

Vegetables & Others (Optional):

  • Potatoes (sliced)
  • Yam (sliced)
  • Kelp
  • Firm tofu

Dipping Sauce:

  • Sesame oil (enough to cover sauce)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • A little sesame seeds
  • Chopped spring onions
  • A few drops of vinegar
  • Chilli (adjust to taste)

How to Make

  • Step 1: Fry the Hot Pot Seasoning
    • Heat a pot over medium heat
    • Add the hot pot seasoning pack
    • Stir-fry until fragrant and fully melted
    • Adjust flavour by adding sesame paste or Lao Gan Ma if desired

  • Step 2: Add Water and Simmer
    • Carefully pour in boiling water
    • Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer

  • Step 3: Prepare the Dipping Sauce
    • In a bowl, add soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame seeds, spring onions, vinegar, and chilli
    • Pour in sesame oil until it covers all ingredients
    • Mix well (sesame oil helps reduce spiciness and coats the food)

  • Step 4: Prepare the Ingredients
    • Arrange beef and lamb slices on a plate
    • Slice potatoes and yams, prepare kelp and firm tofu
    • Avoid leafy vegetables in the spicy pot, as they absorb too much heat

  • Step 5: Cook and Enjoy
    • Add ingredients to the boiling hot pot
    • Cook until just done
    • Dip into the sesame oil sauce and enjoy immediately

Tip: Chongqing hot pot is known for its bold mala flavour. Adjust chilli and sesame oil levels to match your spice tolerance while keeping its rich, aromatic character.

BeiJing HotPot

Beijing hotpot is a traditional Chinese hotpot style known for its light, clear broth that brings out the natural flavours of fresh ingredients. Unlike spicier hotpot varieties, Beijing hotpot focuses on simplicity and balance, often using a mild soup base made from water, ginger, spring onions, and sometimes goji berries or jujubes. This gentle broth allows diners to enjoy the original taste of meats and vegetables without strong seasoning.

Common ingredients include thinly sliced lamb or beef, leafy vegetables, tofu, mushrooms, and glass noodles. Beijing hotpot is typically enjoyed with a variety of dipping sauces, such as sesame paste, garlic, and soy sauce, which add flavour according to personal preference. This hotpot style is popular for family gatherings and is appreciated for its comforting taste and warm, communal dining experience.

Beijing Hot Pot – Home Recipe

Reminder: Before you begin, prepare a copper hot pot. If you don’t have a traditional copper pot, you can use any pot suitable for hot pot cooking and tabletop heating.

Ingredients

Meet (Choose What You Want):

  • Thinly sliced fresh lamb (leg or shoulder preferred; tender and suitable for quick cooking)
  • Beef slices etc.

Hot Pot Base / Broth:

  • Hot pot base (store-bought or homemade with spices such as star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves)
  • Water (for broth)

Vegetables:

  • Napa cabbage
  • Spinach
  • Enoki mushrooms
  • Tofu (choose according to personal preference)

Seasonings / Dipping Sauce Ingredients:

  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Spring onions
  • Coriander
  • Sesame paste
  • Doubanjiang (chilli bean paste)
  • Chilli oil

Others:

  • Noodles, glass noodles, or sliced potatoes (for extra fullness)

How to Make

  • Step 1: Prepare the Copper Pot
    • Place the copper pot on the stove or tabletop burner
    • Heat the pot until it is warm and ready for cooking

  • Step 2: Make the Broth
    • Add water to the pot
    • Put in chicken bones, pork bones, or beef bones
    • Add ginger slices, spring onion segments, and cooking wine
    • Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat and simmer for several hours
    • This creates a rich broth for cooking the lamb

  • Step 3: Prepare the Dipping Sauce
    • Finely mince garlic and ginger
    • Chop spring onions and coriander
    • Place everything into a small bowl
    • Add sesame paste, doubanjiang, and chilli oil
    • Mix well and adjust the proportions to suit your taste

  • Step 4: Prepare the Vegetables
    • Wash all vegetables thoroughly
    • Cut them into bite-sized pieces suitable for hot pot
    • Cut tofu into small cubes
    • Soak noodles or glass noodles in advance until soft

  • Step 5: Cook the Lamb
    • Bring the broth to a rolling boil
    • Pick up lamb slices with chopsticks and swish them quickly in the boiling broth
    • Cook just until the meat changes colour
    • Do not overcook, as this will make the lamb tough

  • Step 6: Cook Vegetables and Other Ingredients
    • After the lamb, cook vegetables and other ingredients in the same broth
    • Adjust cooking time according to each ingredient

  • Step 7: Enjoy
    • Remove cooked lamb and vegetables and place them into a bowl
    • Dip them into the prepared sauce and enjoy while hot

Tip: Copper hot pot cooking is all about freshness and timing—quick cooking keeps the lamb tender and flavourful.

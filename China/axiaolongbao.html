Xiao Long Bao

Xiao Long Bao, also known as soup dumplings, is a famous Chinese dish that originated from the Jiangnan region, especially Shanghai. These delicate dumplings are filled with minced meat and rich, savoury soup, all wrapped in a thin layer of dough. When steamed, the soup melts inside, creating a juicy and flavourful bite.

Xiao Long Bao is traditionally eaten by carefully lifting the dumpling, taking a small bite to sip the soup, and then enjoying the rest with dipping sauce made of vinegar and ginger. Loved for its soft texture and comforting taste, Xiao Long Bao is a popular dish enjoyed in teahouses and restaurants across China.

Dough

  • Flour: 500 g
  • Yeast: 2 g
  • Baking powder: 2 g
  • Fine sugar: 5 g
  • Warm water: 270 g

Filling

  • Pork belly (skin removed): 300 g
  • Scallion: 150 g
  • Ginger: 5 g
  • Dark soy sauce: 2 tbsp
  • Light soy sauce: 1 tbsp
  • Sweet bean sauce: 2 tbsp
  • Five-spice powder: 2 g
  • White pepper powder: 5 g
  • Cooking wine: 1 tbsp

Part A: Make the Dough

  • Step 1: Mix Dry Ingredients
    • Add flour, yeast, baking powder, and sugar into a bowl.

  • Step 2: Add Water
    • Gradually add warm water while stirring with chopsticks until a dough forms.
    • Do not pour all the water in at once‚Äîadjust as needed depending on the flour.

  • Step 3: Knead the Dough
    • Bring the dough together into a large ball.
    • Transfer to a cutting board and knead until smooth.
    • Knead by rotating the dough with one hand while pressing with the other.

  • Step 4: First Proofing
    • Place the dough in a warm place, cover, and let it rest for 2 hours.
    • The dough should double in size and show small air holes.

  • Step 5: Deflate the Dough
    • Knead the dough again to release air bubbles.

  • Step 6: Divide and Roll Wrappers
    • Divide dough into 25 g portions.
    • Flatten each piece and roll into round wrappers.
    • Roll thinner at the edges and thicker in the centre to prevent breaking.

Part B: Prepare the Filling

  • Step 7: Prepare Ingredients
    • Prepare pork belly, scallion, and ginger.
    • Separate scallion whites and greens.

  • Step 8: Grind the Filling
    • Grind pork belly, scallion whites, and ginger together.
    • Do not grind too finely‚Äîslightly coarser than dumpling filling.

  • Step 9: Cook Half the Filling
    • Heat a wok on low heat, add oil.
    • Add dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, cooking wine, sweet bean sauce, five-spice powder, and white pepper.
    • Once boiling, add half of the meat filling.
    • Stir-fry until cooked, then remove and let cool slightly.

  • Step 10: Combine the Filling
    • Add the cooked meat back into the raw meat filling in three batches, mixing well each time.
    • The final filling should be cohesive but not watery.

  • Step 11: Final Seasoning
    • Finely chop scallion greens.
    • Just before wrapping, mix scallion greens and a suitable amount of salt into the filling.

Part C: Wrap & Steam

  • Step 12: Wrap the Xiaolongbao
    • Place a wrapper in your palm.
    • Add an appropriate amount of filling.
    • Pleat and seal into a xiaolongbao shape.

  • Step 13: Second Proofing
    • Line the steamer with oil-proof paper.
    • Place xiaolongbao inside without turning on heat.
    • Cover and let rest for 15 minutes.

  • Step 14: Steam
    • Bring water to a boil over high heat.
    • Steam over medium heat for 13 minutes.
    • Turn off heat and let rest 3 minutes before opening the lid.

Mala

Mala is a bold and flavourful style of Chinese cuisine that comes from Sichuan, China. The word mala means “numbing and spicy,” which describes the unique sensation created by Sichuan peppercorns and dried chillies. This combination gives mala dishes their signature tingling heat that lingers on the tongue.

Mala is commonly used in dishes such as stir-fries, soups, and hotpot, where meats, vegetables, and tofu are cooked in a rich, aromatic sauce. With its strong spices, deep flavours, and exciting taste, mala is especially popular among those who enjoy spicy food and adventurous flavours.To discover how these bold flavours are created, explore to learn more about the ingredients and steps involved in making mala dishes at home.

Mala – Home Recipe

Ingredients

For Mala Sause (Base):

  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 2 tablespoons chilli oil
  • 1–2 tablespoons Sichuan peppercorns
  • 3–4 dried red chillies
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoons ginger (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon doubanjiang (spicy bean paste)
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • ½ teaspoon sugar

Main Ingredients(Choose What You Want):

  • Sliced meat (chicken, beef, or pork)
  • Prawns or fish slices (optional)
  • Firm tofu (cubed)
  • Vegetables (cabbage, lotus root, mushrooms, potatoes)
  • noodles

How to Make

  • Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients
    • Wash and cut all vegetables into bite-sized pieces
    • Slice meat thinly for faster and even cooking
    • Soak glass noodles in warm water until soft

  • Step 2: Make the Mala Base
    • Heat cooking oil in a wok over medium heat
    • Add Sichuan peppercorns and dried chillies, stir until fragrant
    • Add garlic and ginger, stir briefly
    • Mix in chilli oil and doubanjiang, cook until the oil turns red

  • Step 3: Cook the Ingredients
    • Add meat and cook until just done
    • Add tofu and vegetables, stir well to coat with the sauce
    • Add noodles last and mix gently

  • Step 4: Season and Finish
    • Add soy sauce and sugar
    • Stir-fry for another 2–3 minutes until everything is well combined
    • Adjust spice level with extra chilli oil if needed

  • Step 5: Serve
    • Serve hot with steamed rice or enjoy on its own
    • Best eaten immediately for the strongest mala flavour

Tip: The key to great mala is balancing “ma” (numbing) from Sichuan peppercorns and “la” (spicy) from chillies—adjust both to suit your taste.

Nasi Lemak

Nasi Lemak is a popular Southeast Asian dish and is widely regarded as one of Singapore’s most beloved comfort foods. This dish features fragrant rice cooked in coconut milk and pandan leaves, giving it a rich aroma and creamy flavour that forms the heart of the meal.

Nasi Lemak is traditionally served with a variety of accompaniments such as sambal chilli, fried anchovies, peanuts, egg, and protein like chicken or fish. Commonly found at hawker centres and food stalls across Singapore, this dish is loved for its balance of savoury, spicy, and fragrant flavours, making it a favourite among locals and visitors alike.

Nasi Lemak - Home Recipe

Ingredients

Coconut Milk Rice

  • Jasmine rice – 3 cups
  • Coconut cream (thick coconut milk) – ½ cup
  • Water – 3 cups (add extra ½ cup if cooking on the stove)
  • Pandan leaves – 2, knotted
  • Lemongrass – 1 stalk, bruised

Fried Anchovies and Peanuts

  • Cooking oil – 2 teaspoons (divided)
  • Dried anchovies – 1 cup
  • Dry roasted peanuts – 1 cup
  • Sugar – a pinch

Garnish & Serving

  • Cucumber – 1 small, thinly sliced
  • Sambal nasi lemak

How to Make Nasi Lemak (Step-by-Step)

Part A: Cook the Coconut Rice

  • Step 1: Wash the Rice
    • Wash the rice until the water runs clear.
  • Step 2: Cook the Rice (Rice Cooker Method)
    • Add rice, water, coconut cream, pandan leaves, and lemongrass into the rice cooker.
    • Press the cook button and let the rice cook fully.
    • Wait 10 minutes before opening the lid and fluffing the rice.
  • Step 3: Cook the Rice (Stove Method)
    • Bring water, coconut cream, pandan leaves, and lemongrass to a boil in a pot.
    • Add the rice and bring back to a gentle simmer.
    • Cover and cook for 5 minutes.
    • Stir once to prevent the coconut milk from sticking.
    • Cover again and cook for another 5 minutes.
    • Turn off the heat and rest for 10 minutes before fluffing.

Part B: Prepare Fried Anchovies and Peanuts

  • Step 4: Fry the Anchovies
    • Heat 1 teaspoon of oil in a pan.
    • Add dried anchovies and fry until crispy.
    • Add a small pinch of salt and sugar.
    • Remove and set aside.
  • Step 5: Fry the Peanuts
    • Heat the remaining 1 teaspoon of oil in the same pan.
    • Add peanuts and cook until lightly browned.
    • Add a pinch of sugar and salt.
    • Remove from heat and mix well.

Part C: Serve

  • Step 6: Assemble the Dish
    • Portion the coconut rice onto a plate.
    • Serve with sambal nasi lemak on the side.
    • Add fried anchovies, peanuts, and cucumber slices.

Tip: Using coconut cream instead of regular coconut milk gives nasi lemak its signature fragrance without making the rice mushy.

Singapore Cuisine

Laksa

Laksa is a well-known noodle soup in Singapore that is closely associated with Peranakan (Straits Chinese) cuisine, which blends Chinese and Malay culinary influences. This dish features a rich and aromatic coconut-based broth made from laksa paste, spices, and chicken stock, giving it a bold, creamy, and fragrant flavour.

Laksa is traditionally served with vermicelli noodles, tofu puffs, and fresh garnishes, allowing the soup to soak into every ingredient. Commonly enjoyed at hawker centres and casual eateries, laksa is loved for its strong flavours, satisfying texture, and warming spice, making it a favourite comfort food among locals and visitors alike.

Laksa - Home Recipe

Ingredients

Chicken Stock

  • Chicken stock – 2 cups (500 ml)
  • Water – 1 cup (250 ml)
  • Chicken drumsticks – 3

The chicken stock used in this laksa recipe starts with store-bought chicken stock. It is then enhanced by simmering chicken drumsticks in the stock together with water. As the chicken cooks, flavour from the meat, skin, and bones is released into the liquid, creating a richer and more savoury broth for the laksa soup.

Laksa Broth

  • Oil – 1½ tablespoons
  • Garlic – 2 cloves, minced
  • Ginger – 2 cm piece, finely grated
  • Lemongrass – 1 stalk (white part only), grated
  • Bird’s eye chillies – 2, finely chopped
  • Laksa paste – ½ cup (135 g)
  • Coconut milk – 400 ml can
  • Fish sauce – 2 teaspoons

Noodles & Toppings

  • Vermicelli noodles – 50 g (dried)
  • Hokkien noodles – 100 g (optional)
  • Bean sprouts – 80 g
  • Fried tofu puffs – 80 g, cut in half

Laksa Chilli Sauce

  • Sugar – ½ teaspoon
  • Light soy sauce – 1½ teaspoons
  • Garlic – ½ clove, minced
  • Laksa paste – 1½ teaspoons
  • Sriracha sauce – 1 tablespoon
  • Chilli paste (from jar) – 1 tablespoon
  • Vegetable oil – 1 tablespoon

Garnishes

  • Fresh coriander / cilantro
  • Lime wedges
  • Crispy fried shallots (optional)
  • Finely sliced red chilli (optional)

How to Make Laksa (Step-by-Step)

Part A: Prepare the Chicken Stock

  • Step 1: Cook the Chicken Stock
    • Place chicken stock, water, and chicken drumsticks into a saucepan.
    • Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to medium-high.
    • Cook for about 25 minutes until the chicken is tender and liquid reduces by one-third.
  • Step 2: Shred the Chicken
    • Remove chicken from the pot.
    • Discard the skin and bones.
    • Shred the chicken meat and set aside.

Part B: Prepare the Laksa Chilli Sauce

  • Step 3: Mix the Chilli Sauce
    • Combine all chilli sauce ingredients in a bowl.
    • Mix well and set aside for 20 minutes.

Part C: Cook the Laksa Broth

  • Step 4: Sauté Aromatics
    • Heat oil in a pot over medium-low heat.
    • Add garlic and ginger, sauté for about 20 seconds.
    • Add lemongrass and chillies, cook for 1 minute.
  • Step 5: Cook Laksa Paste
    • Add laksa paste and increase heat to medium.
    • Cook for about 2 minutes, stirring until fragrant.
  • Step 6: Simmer the Broth
    • Add chicken stock, coconut milk, fish sauce, and 2 teaspoons of chilli sauce.
    • Cover and simmer for 10 minutes.
    • Add tofu puffs and rest for 5 minutes with the heat off.

Part D: Assemble the Laksa

  • Step 7: Prepare the Noodles
    • Cook vermicelli and hokkien noodles according to packet instructions.
  • Step 8: Serve
    • Divide noodles into bowls.
    • Top with shredded chicken.
    • Pour laksa broth over the noodles.
    • Add bean sprouts and garnishes.
    • Serve with chilli sauce on the side.

Tip: Lightly toasting the rice in rendered chicken fat before cooking allows it to absorb more flavour, resulting in rice that is fragrant and richer than rice cooked with plain water.

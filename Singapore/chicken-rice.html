Chicken Rice

Hainanese Chicken Rice, commonly known as Chicken Rice, is one of Singapore’s most iconic dishes and is widely regarded as a national favourite. Originating from early Chinese immigrants, this dish features tender poached or roasted chicken served with fragrant rice cooked in chicken stock, garlic, and ginger. The simplicity of the ingredients highlights the natural flavours of the chicken and rice, creating a comforting and satisfying meal.

Chicken Rice is traditionally enjoyed with chilli sauce, dark soy sauce, and ginger paste, allowing diners to customise each bite to their taste. Commonly found in hawker centres and food courts across Singapore, this dish is loved for its balance of flavours, soft texture, and affordability, making it a staple meal enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.

Hainanese Chicken Rice - Home Recipe

Ingredients

For the Poached Chicken

  • 1 whole chicken (about 1.5–1.8 kg)
  • Water – enough to almost cover the chicken
  • Ginger – 4 to 5 thick slices
  • Scallions – 2 stalks, cut into halves
  • Salt – 1 tablespoon

For the Rice

  • Jasmine rice – 3 cups
  • Chicken stock – about 4 cups (from poaching the chicken)
  • Chicken fat – 1 to 2 tablespoons (rendered)
  • Garlic – 3 cloves, minced
  • Oil – 1 teaspoon
  • Salt – ½ to 1 teaspoon (to taste)

For the Ginger-Garlic Sauce

  • Ginger – 4-inch piece
  • Garlic – 1 clove
  • Neutral oil – 3 tablespoons
  • Salt – 1 pinch

For the Sweet Dark Soy Sauce

  • Water – ¼ cup
  • Rock sugar – 2.5 tablespoons
  • Dark soy sauce – ¼ cup

For the Chilli Sauce

  • Fresh red chilies – 3
  • Ginger – 1.5-inch piece
  • Garlic – 2 cloves
  • Sesame oil – ¼ teaspoon
  • Salt – ½ teaspoon
  • Sugar – ¼ teaspoon
  • Lime juice – 1 tablespoon
  • Rice vinegar or white vinegar – ½ teaspoon
  • Chicken broth – about 2 tablespoons (adjust for consistency)

How to Make Hainanese Chicken Rice (Step-by-Step)

Part A: Prepare and Cook the Chicken

  • Step 1: Prepare the Chicken
    • Wash the whole chicken thoroughly.
    • Set aside the chicken fat from the back of the cavity.
    • Pat the chicken dry and rub the skin lightly with salt.

  • Step 2: Boil the Chicken
    • Bring a large pot of water to a boil with ginger and scallions.
    • Carefully lower the chicken into the boiling water, breast-side up.
    • Adjust the water level so the chicken breast just pokes above the water.

  • Step 3: Simmer the Chicken
    • Once the water boils again, lift the chicken briefly to drain cold water from the cavity.
    • Return the chicken to the pot and bring the water back to a boil.
    • Lower the heat to a gentle simmer and cover the pot.
    • Cook for about 30–35 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked.

Part B: Ice Bath the Chicken

  • Step 4: Prepare the Ice Bath
    • Prepare a large bowl filled with ice and water.
    • Carefully lift the cooked chicken out of the pot.
    • Drain the water from the cavity.

  • Step 5: Cool the Chicken
    • Lower the chicken into the ice bath.
    • Let it cool for about 15 minutes.
    • Remove, drain completely, and set aside until ready to serve.

Part C: Cook the Rice

  • Step 6: Render Chicken Fat
    • Heat a wok or pan over medium heat.
    • Add the reserved chicken fat with a little oil.
    • Cook until the fat is rendered.

  • Step 7: Fry Garlic and Rice
    • Add garlic and fry briefly until fragrant.
    • Add the uncooked jasmine rice.
    • Stir continuously for about two minutes.

  • Step 8: Cook the Rice
    • Transfer the rice into a rice cooker or pot.
    • Add chicken stock from poaching the chicken instead of water.
    • Add salt and cook until the rice is tender and fragrant.

Part D: Prepare the Sauces

  • Step 9: Make Ginger-Garlic Sauce
    • Grind ginger and garlic until finely minced.
    • Heat oil and gently fry the mixture until aromatic.
    • Add salt to taste and set aside.

  • Step 10: Make Sweet Dark Soy Sauce
    • Heat water and sugar until dissolved and syrupy.
    • Stir in dark soy sauce.
    • Transfer to a serving dish.

  • Step 11: Make Chilli Sauce
    • Grind chilies, ginger, and garlic until finely chopped.
    • Add sesame oil, salt, sugar, lime juice, and vinegar.
    • Mix in chicken broth until the sauce reaches a saucy consistency.

Part E: Serve

  • Step 12: Assemble the Dish
    • Carve the chicken into serving pieces.
    • Serve with fragrant rice and all three sauces.
    • Optionally serve warm chicken broth on the side.

Tip: The key to good Hainanese Chicken Rice lies in the rice—using rendered chicken fat and chicken stock instead of plain water gives the rice its rich aroma and flavour, making it the true highlight of the dish rather than just the chicken.

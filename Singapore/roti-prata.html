Roti Prata

Roti Prata is a popular Indian-inspired flatbread widely enjoyed in Singapore and across Southeast Asia. Introduced by Indian Muslim traders, this dish is known for its thin, flaky layers and crispy exterior with a slightly chewy texture.

Roti Prata is commonly served fresh off the pan with curry or dhal, but it can also be prepared in different variations such as egg prata, coin prata, or sweet dessert prata. Often enjoyed as breakfast, supper, or a quick meal, it remains a favourite comfort food among locals.

Roti Prata - Home Recipe

Ingredients

Dough

  • All-purpose flour – 250 g
  • Salt – 1 teaspoon
  • Sugar – 20 g
  • Hot water – 150 ml
  • Cooking oil – for coating dough and cooking

Serving Suggestions (Plain Prata)

  • Curry
  • Dahl
  • Cheese
  • Sausages
  • Scrambled eggs

How to Make Roti Prata (Step-by-Step)

Part A: Prepare the Dough

  • Step 1: Mix the Ingredients
    • Place flour, salt, sugar, and hot water into a mixing bowl.
    • Knead until a smooth and stretchy dough forms.
  • Step 2: Knead the Dough
    • Knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic.
    • The dough should be stretchy and not sticky.

Part B: Rest the Dough

  • Step 3: Divide and Oil
    • Divide the dough into equal portions.
    • Roll each portion into a round ball.
    • Coat each dough ball lightly with cooking oil.
  • Step 4: Rest the Dough
    • Cover the dough tightly with cling wrap.
    • Rest for at least 2 hours or overnight for best results.

Part C: Stretch and Shape

  • Step 5: Stretch the Dough
    • Oil the work surface lightly.
    • Flatten one dough ball with your palm.
    • Stretch gently from the centre outward until very thin.
  • Step 6: Fold the Dough
    • Rub oil over the surface of the dough.
    • Fold the dough into layers depending on desired shape.

Part D: Cook the Prata

  • Step 7: Pan-Fry
    • Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat with oil.
    • Cook the dough until golden brown and crispy on both sides.
  • Step 8: Fluff the Prata
    • Remove from the pan and let it rest briefly.
    • Fluff and scrunch the prata gently to separate the layers.

Tip: Allowing the dough to rest for at least two hours makes it easier to stretch thin, helping to create crispy, flaky layers without tearing the dough.

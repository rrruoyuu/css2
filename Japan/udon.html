Udon

History of Udon

Udon is a type of thick wheat noodle that has been enjoyed in Japan for centuries. It is believed to have been introduced from China and later adapted into Japanese cuisine. Udon is known for its chewy texture and is commonly served in a light soy-based broth.

How to Make Udon

Ingredients

  • Udon noodles
  • Water
  • Soy sauce
  • Dashi stock
  • Green onions

Steps

  1. Boil udon noodles according to package instructions.
  2. Prepare broth using dashi and soy sauce.
  3. Drain noodles and add to the broth.
  4. Garnish with green onions and serve hot.

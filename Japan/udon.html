Udon

Udon (うどん) is a traditional Japanese thick wheat noodle, prized for its chewy texture and versatility.

Served hot in a light soy-based broth or cold with dipping sauce, udon is a comforting meal enjoyed across Japan throughout the year.

Udon – Home Recipe

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 200g Udon noodles (fresh or dried)
  • 2 cups Dashi stock
  • 2 tsp Soy sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cups Water
  • 1 Green onion, finely chopped

How to Make Udon (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Cook the Noodles

  • Boil udon noodles according to package instructions until tender but chewy.
  • Drain and set aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Broth

  • In a pot, combine dashi stock, water, and soy sauce.
  • Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat.

Step 3: Combine Noodles and Broth

  • Add the cooked noodles to the simmering broth.
  • Heat for 1–2 minutes to warm the noodles through.

Step 4: Garnish and Serve

  • Transfer udon to serving bowls.
  • Garnish with chopped green onions and serve hot.

Tip: For extra flavor, add toppings like tempura, soft-boiled egg, or spinach. Cold udon served with dipping sauce is a refreshing option in summer.

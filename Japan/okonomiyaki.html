Okonomiyaki

Okonomiyaki (お好み焼き) is a popular and comforting Japanese savoury pancake, especially associated with Osaka and Hiroshima, where it is enjoyed as a casual street food or shared meal. Known for its rich, hearty flavour and crisp-on-the-outside, soft-on-the-inside texture, it is a dish that feels both filling and fun to eat.

The name literally means “grilled as you like it,” highlighting its versatility and customizable nature. Made with a batter mixed with shredded cabbage, okonomiyaki can include ingredients such as pork, seafood, or cheese, and is cooked on a hot griddle. It is typically finished with a generous drizzle of savoury sauce, mayonnaise, and dried toppings, creating a balanced blend of sweet, salty, and umami flavours.

Okonomiyaki – Home Recipe

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 4–6 slices of pork belly or bacon
  • 1–2 tablespoon(s) water (adjust for batter consistency)

Toppings

  • Okonomiyaki sauce
  • Japanese mayonnaise
  • Dried bonito flakes (optional)
  • Aonori seaweed powder (optional)

How to Make Okonomiyaki (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Prepare the Batter

  • In a bowl, mix flour, eggs, chopped cabbage, and water until you get a thick but pourable batter.

Step 2: Cook the Pancake

  • Heat a lightly oiled pan over medium heat.
  • Pour the batter into the pan and shape into a round pancake.
  • Cook until the bottom is golden brown, about 3–4 minutes.

Step 3: Add Pork and Flip

  • Place pork slices on top of the pancake.
  • Carefully flip the pancake and cook for another 3–4 minutes until the pork is cooked and the pancake is golden on both sides.

Step 4: Add Toppings and Serve

  • Transfer the pancake to a plate.
  • Drizzle okonomiyaki sauce and Japanese mayonnaise on top.
  • Sprinkle with bonito flakes and aonori if desired.
  • Serve hot.

Tip: Feel free to customize the filling with shrimp, squid, or cheese to create your own version of Okonomiyaki. Adjust the thickness of the batter to suit your preference.

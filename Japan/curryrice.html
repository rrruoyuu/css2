Curry Rice

History of Curry Rice

Japanese curry rice was introduced to Japan during the late 19th century through the British Navy. Originally based on Indian curry, it was adapted to suit Japanese tastes, becoming thicker, milder, and slightly sweet. Over time, curry rice became a popular home-cooked meal and a staple in Japanese households, school lunches, and restaurants.

How to Make Curry Rice

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked Japanese rice
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 1 carrot (chopped)
  • 1 potato (chopped)
  • 200g chicken, beef, or pork
  • 1 box Japanese curry roux
  • 2 cups water
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pot and sauté onions until soft.
  2. Add meat and cook until browned.
  3. Add carrots, potatoes, and water. Simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Turn off heat, add curry roux, and stir until dissolved.
  5. Simmer again for 10 minutes until thick.
  6. Serve hot over steamed rice.

Miso Soup

Miso soup (味噌汁) is a classic and beloved Japanese staple, valued for its comforting warmth and deep savoury umami flavour. Light yet nourishing, it is often one of the first dishes served at the table and is enjoyed by people of all ages as part of everyday meals.

This simple soup is made by dissolving fermented soybean paste (miso) into dashi stock, creating a gently salty and aromatic broth. Common ingredients such as tofu, seaweed, and spring onions are added for texture and balance. Frequently enjoyed in Japanese households, miso soup is appreciated not only for its taste but also for its nutritional value and soothing, home-cooked feel.

Miso Soup – Home Recipe

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 2 cups Dashi stock
  • 2 Tbsp Miso paste (adjust to taste)
  • 50g Tofu, cut into small cubes
  • 1 Tbsp Dried wakame seaweed
  • 1 Green onion, finely chopped

How to Make Miso Soup (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Heat the Stock

  • Pour dashi stock into a pot and bring it to a gentle simmer over medium heat.

Step 2: Add Tofu and Seaweed

  • Add tofu cubes and dried wakame seaweed to the simmering stock.

Step 3: Dissolve Miso Paste

  • Turn off the heat to prevent boiling, then gradually dissolve miso paste into the soup, stirring gently.

Step 4: Garnish and Serve

  • Top with finely chopped green onions and serve immediately while warm.

Tip: Avoid boiling miso after adding it, as high heat can reduce its delicate flavor and kill beneficial probiotics. You can also add mushrooms or spinach for extra nutrition.

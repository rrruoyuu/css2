Bite of Asia

Takoyaki

Takoyaki (たこ焼き) is a beloved Japanese street food originating from Osaka, known for its bite-sized shape and comforting, savoury taste. Often enjoyed fresh off the griddle at street stalls and festivals, it is a popular snack that’s meant to be shared and eaten while hot.

These round balls are made from a light batter filled with pieces of octopus, then cooked until crispy on the outside and soft and fluffy on the inside. Takoyaki is typically topped with a rich savoury sauce, mayonnaise, and dancing bonito flakes, creating a delicious combination of umami, sweetness, and texture in every bite.

Takoyaki – Home Recipe

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 1 cup Takoyaki batter (store-bought or homemade)
  • 50g cooked octopus, cut into small pieces
  • 2–3 Green onions, finely chopped

Toppings

  • Takoyaki sauce
  • Japanese mayonnaise
  • Dried bonito flakes (optional)
  • Aonori seaweed powder (optional)

How to Make Takoyaki (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Preheat the Pan

  • Heat a takoyaki pan over medium heat and lightly oil each cavity.

Step 2: Pour the Batter

  • Pour the batter into each cavity until almost full.

Step 3: Add Filling

  • Place a piece of octopus and some chopped green onions into each ball.

Step 4: Cook and Turn

  • Cook until the bottom is golden brown, then use skewers to turn the balls, rotating until they form round shapes and are cooked through.

Step 5: Add Toppings and Serve

  • Remove takoyaki from the pan and place on a plate.
  • Top with takoyaki sauce, mayonnaise, bonito flakes, and aonori.
  • Serve immediately while hot.

Tip: Keep turning the takoyaki carefully to ensure they cook evenly and become perfectly round. You can also add cheese or tempura scraps for extra flavor.
